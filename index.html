About the Local

We're Not the Act.
We're What Makes It Possible.

When a touring production rolls into Omaha, our members are the ones who make it work. We hang the lights, run the cables, rig the motors, build the stage, and run the sound — then tear it all down and do it again.

We're not the faces you see on stage. We're the reason the show happens at all — every time, at every venue, without fail. IATSE Local 42 has been doing this work in Omaha since 1896.

As part of the International Alliance of Theatrical Stage Employees, our members carry union cards recognized across the country — with wages, benefits, and working conditions negotiated to protect the people who do the work.

How It Works

From Truck to Curtain

01

Load In

Trucks arrive. Our crew unloads every case, piece of truss, and speaker cabinet — then builds the entire production from scratch in a matter of hours. What looks like chaos has a plan down to the last bolt.

02

Show Call

Showtime. Riggers overhead, electricians on follow spots, audio and video techs running the board — every department working in sync to make sure the audience sees a perfect show.

03

Strike

When the last note plays, we tear it all down, pack the trucks, and send the tour on its way — ready to do it again in the next city. We're already gone before the audience reaches the parking lot.
